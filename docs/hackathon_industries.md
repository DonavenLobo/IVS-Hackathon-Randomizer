# The definitive hackathon industry list: 275 sub-industries across 24 sectors

**This master list compiles 275 distinct, hyper-specific sub-industries drawn from four formal classification systems (GICS, NAICS, ICB, ISIC), startup ecosystem taxonomies (Y Combinator, Crunchbase, PitchBook, CB Insights), and frontier-industry research.** Every entry is specific enough to meaningfully constrain a hackathon design challenge — participants who draw "orbital debris removal" face a radically different problem space than those who draw "carbon credit marketplaces." The list spans the full global economy, from century-old heavy industries to sectors that barely existed before 2023.

The 24 sectors below are organized from traditional/physical industries through digital/frontier ones. Each contains **8–15 sub-industries** with zero overlap between entries. The total count of **275** provides ample randomization headroom for monthly hackathons running years without repetition.

## How this list was built

This taxonomy synthesizes **four formal classification systems** and **seven startup ecosystem sources** to eliminate the blind spots each has individually. GICS and ICB contributed the backbone of traditional sectors — financials, energy, materials, industrials — but both undercount technology sub-segments and ignore frontier industries entirely. NAICS added granularity in agriculture, government, construction, and specific service industries that financial classifications under-represent. ISIC confirmed international coverage.

The startup ecosystem sources filled critical gaps. **Crunchbase's 700+ industry tags** revealed categories like "pet technology" and "restaurant technology" that formal systems lump into generic buckets. **PitchBook's 54 verticals** contributed distinct entries like FemTech, micromobility, and cannabis. **CB Insights market maps** surfaced ultra-specific segments like embedded insurance and retail media networks. **Y Combinator's request-for-startups categories** flagged AI agents and vertical AI as distinct investment categories. Frontier-industry research identified **133 emerging sub-industries** across space tech, synthetic biology, quantum computing, and other domains that lack NAICS codes entirely (the next NAICS revision isn't until 2027).

Deduplication removed approximately **160 overlapping entries** across systems. Every remaining sub-industry was tested against a specificity threshold: could two hackathon teams drawing this category reasonably design different but category-appropriate solutions? Entries like "insurance" failed (too broad); "parametric crop insurance" was too narrow. The sweet spot — entries like **"carbon credit marketplaces"** or **"autonomous delivery drones"** — gives teams a clear domain without prescribing a single solution.
